Token Supply

The Saito Network has 7bb SAITO tokens in circulation as per the chart below. Because Saito is a non-inflationary token, the only way to get tokens is to purchase them from other holders or run network servers and earn them in exchange for routing transaction fees into the network. This makes Saito tokenomics fundamentally different than proof-of-work and proof-of-stake mechanisms that rely on token inflation to pay for network services and use issuance and lock-up games to debase holders.

A portion of the Saito tokens which exist are circulating in the form of ERC20 / BEP20 tokens that holders can migrate on-chain as needed and which exist for integration with other parts of the cryptocurrency ecosystem. The project supports a bridge that holders can use to migrate these tokens to mainnet at any time. Please note that this is the only officially-supported way of migrating tokens to mainnet. If someone asks you to use another method it is likely a scam.

How to Purchase SAITO

Saito is circulating both a wrapped (ERC20, BRC20) and native on-chain token. If you hold ERC20 or BSC Saito you can earn interest from liquidity provision (a form of staking) on off-chain exchanges. On-chain token balances are eligible for ATR rewards through the Saito Consensus mechanism in an amount not in excess of 5 percent annually. This subsidy for on-chain balance is intended to encourage migration and support on-chain transaction growth.

Token Persistence Curve

The token persistence threshold is currently at 18.5k Saito.

In order to ensure stable development and help our team confirm the network is properly handling the rebroadcasting of on-chain assets, token migration is limited by our token persistence curve below. This graph shows the minimum amount of $SAITO that is required to be shifted to the network in order to take advantage of migration. Users who migrate should also be aware that UTXO with less than this specific amount of $SAITO will also be at risk of funds loss / reaping on network reset until token persistence is fully-supported at the end of 2025 and the network shifts into permanent operation.
